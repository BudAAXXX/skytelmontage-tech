What we do

SkyTel Montage focuses on the installation, modernisation and service of mobile and radio networks in Germany. We deliver complete antenna and transmission systems on towers, rooftops and special structures ‚Äì from steelwork and cable routing through installation and testing of active equipment to final photo documentation and handover reports.

The work combines rope-access techniques, electrical qualification and project documentation according to German and Czech standards. Typical scopes include greenfield roll-out, swap and upgrade programmes and corrective or emergency maintenance.

  • Photo documentation, punch list handling and as-built reports in DE/EN/CZ.

Team and work organisation

SkyTel Montage works with small, stable crews that combine electrical qualification, PSAgA rope-access training and hands-on experience from German telecom projects. Each crew is structured to handle the full installation cycle ‚Äì from site preparation to photo documentation and handover reports.

Roles in the crew

  • Lead technician / team lead: electrical qualification (NV 194/2022 ¬ß6/¬ß7 or equivalent Elektrofachkraft), coordination of works, responsibility for HSE (BOZP/DGUV) and quality control.
  • Rope-access technician (L2): assembly of steel and antenna structures, cable routing, installation of anchor points and support with rescue procedures.
  • Rope-access technician / rigger: finishing work, clean cable routing and labelling, photo documentation and detailed checks according to project standards.

What is billed per team member

  • Day rate: professional work on site, preparation, documentation and standard tools.
  • Travel costs: share of van, fuel, tolls and parking, allocated according to project and crew size.
  • Per diems & accommodation: usually according to German / Czech rates, invoiced as agreed.
  • Special equipment: optional surcharges for specific measurement and test equipment (e.g. EMF meters, electrical testers, special rescue kits) requested by the project.

Payment options

  • Invoicing via Czech limited company (EU VAT): invoicing in EUR or CZK, standard payment terms.
  • Milestone-based billing: project split into phases such as build, commissioning and final acceptance.
  • Monthly summary invoices: one consolidated invoice per month for all sites and days worked.
